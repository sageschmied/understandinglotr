The Origins of Evil

Understanding the Malicious Background of Sauron

Posted by Sage on March 31st, 2017

Sauron. He’s big, he’s bad, he’s ugly. And not to mention the literal eye in the sky.

But I bet you’ve wondered while watched the Lord of the Rings films, who is this dude? Where did he come from? How come he’s so bad and big? Well I may have some answers for you.

Sauron, is he a guy? Is he an elf? What the heck is he?? Sauron is actually of the race of Maia/Maiar, (and so are Balrogs by the way, you know, big fire dude- YOU SHALL NOT PASS!), but you can learn more about the Maiar/Maia in the post on races in LOTR.

Essentially, Sauron existed before the world which Middle Earth exists in was created. He’s very evil, and powerful, because of his race he can exist in any sort of form he wishes and can basically do major damage. Sauron though, learned everything he knew from the original bad boy- Melkor/Melko/Morgoth.

Melkor The Orignal Bad Guy

Melkor/Melko/Morgoth was of the Valar, the higher up, more powerful versions of the Maiar, and were very active in the creating of the world. Melkor was known as “he who arises in might”, or Morgoth “the dark enemy of the world” and Sauron was his second in command basically.

This dude was even, bigger and meaner and uglier than Sauron and he did everything he could to screw up the world in the beginning. Melkor was filled with anger, jealousy, arrogance etc. basically all the characteristics of an evil, evil dude.

"From splendour he fell through arrogance to contempt for all things save himself, a spirit wasteful and pitiless" Melkor, as described in The Simarillion p. 23

Melkor, fortunately met his demise and was literally kicked out of the world, never to be seen again in Middle Earth. Sauron however, managed to escape from the forces of good, and went on to terrorize the characters we know and love from the LOTR films

Sauron is also known as a “Gorthaur the Cruel,” and was there every step of the way and through every evil deed that Melkor did (And Melkor created Orcs y’all). In the end, Sauron rose like a shadow and followed Melkor’s path down to the Void of the world.
